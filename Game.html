  • Acid Rain

    Cause: Acid rain forms when sulfur dioxide (SO2) and nitrogen oxides (NOx) are released into the atmosphere by burning fossil fuels, such as coal and oil, in power plants, industrial processes, and vehicles. These pollutants mix with water vapor in the atmosphere, creating sulfuric and nitric acids that fall as acid rain, damaging plants, aquatic life .




  • Deforestation

    Cause:

    Deforestation is caused by clearing forests for agriculture, logging, and urban development. This leads to habitat loss, disruption of ecosystems, and increased carbon emissions, contributing to climate change.

  • Global Warming

    Cause:

    Global warming occurs due to the release of greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide and methane, primarily from burning fossil fuels. This leads to an increase in Earthâ€™s average temperature, melting ice caps, rising sea levels, and extreme weather events.

  • air pollution

    Cause:

    Household combustion devices, motor vehicles, industrial facilities and forest fires are common sources of air pollution. Pollutants of major public health concern include particulate matter, carbon monoxide, ozone, nitrogen dioxide and sulfur dioxide

  • Water Pollution

    Cause:

    Water contamination occurs from chemical runoff from industries, agricultural pesticides, untreated sewage, plastic waste, and oil spills. These pollutants are introduced into rivers, lakes, and oceans, making the water unsafe for aquatic life and human consumption.

  • Land Pollution

    Cause:

    Land pollution results from the improper disposal of waste, including plastics, chemicals, and industrial byproducts. Waste dumped in landfills or left on the ground contaminates the soil, harming plant life, reducing agricultural productivity.

  • Overpopulation

    Cause:

    With the global population growing rapidly, the demand for food, water, and energy increases. This leads to deforestation, pollution, and overuse of natural resources. Unsustainable consumption and wasteful living contribute to the degradation of the environment.

  • Water Scarcity

    Cause:

    Overuse of water resources, particularly in agriculture, combined with pollution, climate change, and overpopulation, has led to the depletion of freshwater supplies. Droughts, changing weather patterns, and inefficient water management systems further contribute to water scarcity, affecting agriculture, drinking water, and sanitation.

  • Noise Pollution

    Cause:

    Noise pollution comes from traffic, industrial activities, construction, and loud events. It can harm human health, causing stress, sleep disturbances, and hearing loss.

  • Plastic Pollution

    Cause:

    The widespread use of single-use plastics, such as bottles, bags, and packaging, combined with improper disposal practices, leads to plastic waste accumulating in landfills, rivers, oceans, and streets. Plastics take hundreds of years to break down, causing long-term environmental harm, especially to marine life.
